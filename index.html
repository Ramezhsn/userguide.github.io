User Guide

How to use the Lebanon Multi‑Hazard & Climate Platform

This guide explains what each page represents, how to navigate the maps, and how to interpret the statistics and charts. Designed for planners, responders, and analysts working on risk‑informed decision‑making.

1) Overview (Home)

Purpose. Introduces the platform and its data coverage. Use it to understand scope and jump to modules.

Coverage. Municipalities 1,627 • Districts 26 • Governorates 8.
Map. Pan/zoom to explore administrative areas. Use the top menu to open analytical pages.
Partners. LRC, ICRC, Climate Centre, and 510 jointly developed this data‑driven platform.

How to use

  • Start from the top red menu to open Climate Profile, EVCA Results, or Impact Chains.
  • Hover map features for quick info; follow Read the story for context.

2) Hazard & Exposure + Climate Drivers

Three synchronized views help you contrast current exposure, historical climate, and projected change.

Left: Hazard & Exposure

  • Points and polygons show hazard events and exposed assets.
  • Use the search bar to jump to a municipality or district.

Center: Historic Drivers

  • Historic indicators such as SPI, precipitation, humidity.
  • Use layers to compare spatial patterns across Lebanon.

Right: Projected Change

  • Future (2021–2050) changes in climate drivers.
  • Read the table for per‑area values: SPI, humidity, temperatures, hot‑day counts.
Reading the table (quick primer)

Historic vs. Future columns show baseline and projection. Δ absolute / Δ% express the magnitude of change. SPI < 0 indicates drier conditions; positive values indicate wetter conditions.

3) Climate Profile – Lebanon

Interactive map, charts, and narratives by municipality for a selected climate variable.

Controls

  • Variable: temperature (summer mean), humidity, precipitation, etc.
  • Period: Historic vs. Future.
  • Filters: Governorate, District, Municipality.

Outputs

  • Choropleth Map of municipalities for the chosen variable.
  • Time‑step Chart comparing historic vs. future.
  • Top‑10 Change list ranked by Δ (largest shifts).
  • Auto‑Summary (plain‑language interpretation for the selected area).
How to interpret color ramps

Cool colors (greens/blues) generally mean lower values; warm colors (yellows/reds) mean higher values. Always confirm the legend units (°C, %, index).

4) EVCA Results

Community‑level assessments combining hazards, vulnerabilities, capacities, and priorities.

Selectors

  • Choose Governorate → District → Municipality.
  • Select a Resilience Dimension to view details.

Hazards

  • Main hazards (e.g., Forest Fire, Winter Storm, Soil Erosion) with cause/origin, warning signs, onset, frequency.
  • Use this to plan early‑warning, mitigation, and preparedness.

What you see

  • Radar (Community Score) across 10 dimensions.
  • Vulnerability & Capacity narratives for the selected dimension.
  • Priorities: suggested focus areas for action.
  • Map: highlighted municipality for spatial context.

5) Impact Chains

Interactive cause–effect diagrams show how hazards propagate through exposure and vulnerability into sectoral impacts, and where adaptation/mitigation and capacity measures can break the chain.

What you can do on this page

  • Filter chips at the top (e.g., Natural Hazard, Man‑made hazard, Impact, Capacity, Vulnerability, External driver, Exposed system, Adaptation/mitigation measure) to highlight relevant nodes.
  • Left sidebar to switch context (e.g., Arsal, Blue Line, North‑Akkar).
  • Zoom / pan the canvas; use the toolbar for fit‑to‑screen or center.
  • Click any node to open the narrative panel (left) with a definition, local context, references, and associated Resilience Dimension tags.

How to read the graph

  • Colours represent types: light‑blue = Natural hazard, steel‑blue = Man‑made hazard, grey = Impact, mint = Capacity, orange = Exposed system, teal = External driver.
  • Arrow styles: green = causes, red = mitigates, blue = affects/relates.
  • Legend at the bottom repeats the mapping between colours and line types.

Typical workflows

  1. Select a context from the sidebar (e.g., Blue Line).
  2. Use filter chips to focus on a subset (e.g., Impact + Capacity).
  3. Click a node (e.g., Moving mines or Uneven distributed aid) to read the narrative, sources, and resilience dimensions.
  4. Trace upstream causes and downstream impacts to identify intervention points (awareness sessions, contingency planning, funding for emergency centres, etc.).

6) Navigation Tips

  • Use the red top menu to switch modules; use in‑map search bars to jump to places.
  • Hover features for pop‑ups; use mouse wheel or touch to zoom; drag to pan.
  • Compare Historic vs Future to understand trends; export screenshots for reports.
Historic vs. Future Δ absolute / Δ% SPI • Temperature • Humidity

Glossary & Notes

  • SPI (Standardized Precipitation Index): negative → drier; positive → wetter vs baseline.
  • Δ (Delta): change between future and historic values; may be shown as °C, %, or index units.
  • EVCA: Enhanced Vulnerability and Capacity Assessment—community driven assessment framework.
